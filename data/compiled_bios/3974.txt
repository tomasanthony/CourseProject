Professor Smith earned his BS, MS, and PhD degrees from the University of Central Florida. He worked for AT&amp;T for fifteen years, in several different IT capacities, during which time his PhD studies were sponsored by AT&amp;Ts Doctoral Support Program. His research spans elements of theoretical and experimental computer science, in the area of parallel and distributed computation. His interests include models of concurrency, bioinformatics (specifically, computational phylogeny), and programming languages (semantics, paradigms, and unifying theories).
