Dr. Agichtein founded and leads the Emory Intelligent Information Access Laboratory (IR Lab). Eugene's general research interests are in web search and information retrieval, text and data mining, and human-computer interaction, with a key focus on medical informatics and social media applications. Eugene received a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Columbia University, and a B.S. in Engineering from The Cooper Union. Eugene is a Sloan Research Fellow, a member of the DARPA Computer Science Study Group, and a recipient of four best paper awards, most recently at the SIGIR 2011 and WSDM 2014 conferences. His work has been supported by grants from DARPA, the National Institutes of Health, and the National Science Foundation, and by gifts from Cisco, Google, HP Labs, Microsoft, Motorola, the Sloan Foundation, and Yahoo!
