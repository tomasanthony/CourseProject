Keith M. Chugg received the B.S. degree (high distinction) in Engineering from Harvey Mudd College, Claremont, CA in 1989 and the M.S.~and Ph.D.~degrees in Electrical Engineering (EE) from the University of Southern California (USC), Los Angeles, CA in 1990 and 1995, respectively.  During the 1995-1996 academic year he was an Assistant Professor with the Electrical and Computer Engineering Dept., The University of Arizona, Tucson, AZ.  In 1996 he joined the  EE Dept. at USC in 1996 where he is currently a Professor.   He is a co-founder of TrellisWare Technologies, Inc.,  where he is Chief Scientist.  He has served as an associate  editor for the IEEE Transactions on Communications and was Program Co-Chair for the Communication Theory Symposium at Globecom 2002.    He received the Fred W.~Ellersick award for the best unclassified paper at MILCOM 2003 and the ASEE Frederick Emmons Terman Award in 2008. Research SummaryProfessor Chugg's research interests are in the general areas of signal design, signal processing, detection, estimation, and machine learning.
