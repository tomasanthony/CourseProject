Former students David Kohlbrenner (Ph.D. 2018) UC Berkeley (postdoc) Wilson Lian (Ph.D. 2016) Google Jacob Maskiewicz (M.S. 2016) Facebook Keaton Mowery (Ph.D. 2015) Apple Daniel Cashman (M.S. 2013) Google Stephen Checkoway (Ph.D. 2012) Johns Hopkins UI Chicago Aishwarya Venkataraman (M.S. 2012) Stack iQ Grace Wang (M.S. 2010) Qualcomm Brief biography Hovav Shacham joined the University of Texas at Austin in 2018. His research interests are in applied cryptography, systems security, privacy-enhancing technologies, and technology policy. Shacham was a student at Stanford and a postdoctoral fellow at the Weizmann Institute. From 2007 to 2018, he was on the faculty at the University of California, San Diego. He received the 2017 ACM CCS Test-of-Time Award for his 2007 paper that introduced return-oriented programming. Shacham took part in Californias 2007 Top-to-Bottom voting systems review and served on the advisory committee for Californias 201113 post-election risk-limiting audit pilot program. His work has been cited by the Federal Trade Commission, the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, and the RAND Corporation.
