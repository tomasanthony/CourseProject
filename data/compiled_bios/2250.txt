He received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from Stony Brook University in 2012. He received his B.S. in Computer Science & Applied Mathematics and Statistics from Stony Brook University in 2006. Research Joseph Marino's research projects focus on visualization and computer graphics algorithms for medical imaging applications, including visualization and analysis for pancreatic and prostate cancer screening, as well as enhancements for virtual colonoscopy. Awards Joseph Marino is a recipient of the 2016 Long Island Technology Hall of Fame Patent Award in the category of Innovation in Health and the Individual.
