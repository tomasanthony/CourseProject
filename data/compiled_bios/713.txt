I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science at San Francisco State University . I am also a faculty member in the Center for Computing for Life Sciences (CCLS) at SFSU. I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from The Ohio State University in Summer 2006 under the guidance of Dr. Srinivasan Parthasarathy . M y research interests are primarily in the area of D ata Mining and Text Analytics . Presently, I am focusing on the following projects: - Biomedical text mining to learn the relationships between foods, diseases and genes, aiming to build interactive food-centric networks to promote better human health. - Analyzing clinical notes to extract a patient's family history. - Building an interactive system to analyze a variety of Microarray data in different areas of biology. Click here to read about and download XMAS, a software system designed to analyze and explore time series Microarray data. - Structural analysis for protein folding data.
